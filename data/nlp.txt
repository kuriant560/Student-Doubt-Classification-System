Natural Language Processing (NLP) focuses on the interaction between computers and human language, particularly how to program computers to process and analyze large amounts of natural language data.
Tokenization is the process of breaking down a large body of text into smaller components such as words or sentences, called tokens.
A Bag-of-Words (BoW) model represents text data by counting the frequency of words in a document but ignoring order or structure.
TF-IDF stands for Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency. It evaluates how relevant a word is to a document in a collection of documents by weighing word frequency against document frequency.
Stop words are commonly used words (such as 'the', 'a', 'an', 'in') that a search engine has been programmed to ignore, both when indexing entries for searching and when retrieving them as the result of a search query.
Embeddings such as Word2Vec and GloVe represent words as dense mathematical vectors, capturing semantic meaning and context.
